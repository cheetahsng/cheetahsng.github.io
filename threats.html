Human Encroachment
is a huge threat when it comes to cheetahs. It is their greatest threat and doesn’t look like it’s going away any sooner.
Cheetahs are also killed by hunters because they are viewed as a threat to livestock.
Cub mortality
is high due to predation by carnivores like lions, hyenas and other carnivores that are in competition with the cheetah.
The cheetah is the oldest big cat. But statistics aren’t helping their survival. An estimate states that just about
6,674
cheetah exist in their stronghold, the wild.
80%
is the percentage of their historic range that they have lost.
There is no record of a cheetah ever killing a human. In fact, cheetah cubs are unusually easy to tame, which has led them to be smuggled for pets.
Genetic Inbreeding
is one of the most major problems faced by cheetahs. This leads to several abnormalities.
