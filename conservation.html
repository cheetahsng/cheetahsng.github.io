Conservation of Cheetahs
SCROLL DOWN TO LEARN WHAT IS BEING DONE TO CONSERVE CHEETAS
The most endangered big cat in Africa
is the cheetah.
90%
is how much the cheetah population has decreased in the past 100 years.
The Cheetah population covers 21 countries of the world.
Cheetah Conservation Fund
Founded in 1990, the CCF in Namibia is the world’s leading organization dedicated to saving the cheetah in the wild.
Zoological Society of London
Although the institution of the ZSL is located in London, Cheetah conservation in Tanzania has been a major focus for ZSL since 1991.
Mara Cheetah Project
Realising that the cheetah’s population is slowly decreasing, the Kenya Wildlife Trust set up the Mara Cheetah Project in order to determine the threats that cheetahs face.
Namibian farmers are also increasingly using the implementation of predator-friendly livestock management techniques.
4th December
is the International Cheetah day.
The Maasai Mara Game Reserve in Kenya, the Serengeti National Park in Tanzania, and the Kruger National Park in South Africa are some national parks that help in the conservation of cheetahs.
The Nat Geo Big Cats Initiative
is an amazing program by National Geographic to help cheetahs and other big cats survive.
