--- layout: default title: Subspecies ---

The Asiatic Cheetah
Native to the Arabian peninsula where it split up with the cheetahs from Africa, this marvel of evolution is rated Critically Endangered, with a population of less than 50 in the wild.
The East African Cheetah
Adapted to the east African savannah, there are some 1,960 to 2,572 mature east African cheetahs. Its length can go up to 135 cm.
The Northeast African Cheetah
It gives up its meal to spotted and striped hyenas. It prefers the Soemmerring Gazelle as its prey. The depletion of the gazelle has caused near extinction in Sudan.
The Saharan Cheetah
It is a critically endangered subspecies of the cheetah. Their main prey are antelopes which have adapted to the arid areas that they live in. A sighting is rare with their dwindling population, but with intricate camera traps, is possible.
The South African Cheetah
Its survival is ensured by an influx of evolutionary marvels and conservational efforts from both organisations and individuals. Like the other subspieces, this cheetah also lives in the savannah, grasslands and shrub lands.
